Up to 30% project time lost to poor requirements management

• Inadequate or unclear requirements often lead to frequent changes and rework
• Each iteration of rework adds time to the development process

• Poorly managed requirements can result in misunderstandings between stakeholders and development teams
• This can lead to the need for revisions and adjustments, which extend the timeline

• Unclear requirements may contribute to scope creep, where additional features or changes are introduced during development
• This can substantially increase the workload and time required

• Incomplete or ambiguous requirements can lead to inadequately tested features
• Testing becomes more time-consuming as issues surface late in the development process

• If key decisions related to requirements are delayed, it can stall progress on the project, affecting subsequent development phases

• Poorly managed requirements may result in a product that doesn't meet customer expectations
• Addressing customer dissatisfaction often involves further modifications and extensions to the development timeline
